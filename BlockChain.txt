What is a Wallet in Cryptocurrency?
A crypto wallet is a digital tool that allows users to store, send, and receive cryptocurrencies securely. It manages private and public keys

Public Key: Similar to a bank account number; it's shared with others to receive funds.
Private Key: A secret key used to sign transactions and prove ownership of the funds. If lost, access to the wallet is lost forever.
Wallets do not store actual cryptocurrencies. Instead, they store private keys that allow access to assets stored on the blockchain.



2. Types of Crypto Wallets
A. Hot Wallets (Online & Internet-Connected)
These wallets are connected to the internet, making them easy to use but more vulnerable to hacking.

old Wallets (Offline & More Secure)
These wallets are not connected to the internet, making them highly secure against cyberattacks.

Hardware Wallets: Ledger Nano S, Trezor

Paper Wallets: A printed QR code representing a private key



What are Gas Fees?
Gas fees are the cost of processing transactions on a blockchain. These fees go to miners/validators who confirm transactions.
